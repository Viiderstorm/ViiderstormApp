Checkpoint 3
Basic Shading - Jason Curry

Overview

In this section, we're going to add some basic shading using the Phong Illumination Model.

The idea here is to begin adding shadow effects, highlights, and gradients to our computer generated scenes to produce a more realistic looking image. The Phong Illumination Model manages this by solving a set of equations for each of the different forms of light in our scene:

  • Ambient Light: The sum of light that comes into a point on a surface from all directions.
    - It provides a basic underlying color for the object's surface and their shadows.
  • Diffuse Light: The light that comes in at a particular angle toward a point on a surface and reflects in many different directions.
    - It provides a gradient nice effect given the angle of the light source from the surface normal.
  • Specular Light: The light that comes in at a particular angle toward a point and reflects in the opposite angle between the surface normal and view direction.
    - It provides the shininess factor given the viewing angle.
